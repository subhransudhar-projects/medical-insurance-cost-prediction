# Stage 4 — Financial Impact Estimates (illustrative)

Scaled to a **10,000-member book** (2,049 smokers, 1,085 obese smokers). Figures use dataset segment economics + explicit conservative assumptions. **Planning estimates, not guarantees.**

| Initiative | Target | Realized $/yr | Program $/yr | Net $/yr |
|---|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Risk-based repricing (smoker x BMI) | 1,085 obese smokers | $13,141,099 | $0 | **$13,141,099** |
| Smoking cessation program | 2,049 smokers | $2,419,274 | $1,024,682 | **$1,394,592** |
| Weight mgmt (obese smokers only) | 1,085 obese smokers | $1,642,637 | $867,614 | **$775,023** |

**Total conservative net annual impact: ~$15,310,715 per 10,000 members (~$1,531 per member).**

### Assumptions

- **Risk-based repricing (smoker x BMI):** recover 60% of the ~$20k/head under-pricing.
- **Smoking cessation program:** 10% quit, 50% yr-1 realization, $500/enrollee.
- **Weight mgmt (obese smokers only):** 15% drop below BMI30, 50% realization, $800/enrollee.

### Prioritization (by ROI)
1. **Risk-based repricing** — near-zero cost, immediate; fixes structural under-pricing.
2. **Smoking cessation** — largest addressable pool; ROI positive even at a conservative 10% quit rate.
3. **Targeted weight management** — high per-head value but smaller eligible population.
